Who were the signers

Famous signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Primary author of the Declaration. Later became the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Leading advocate for independence in the Continental Congress. Became the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
World-renowned inventor, diplomat, and statesman. Key figure in securing French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress. Known for his large, bold signature, which became iconic.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Prominent organizer of resistance, including the Boston Tea Party. Cousin of John Adams and a key revolutionary figure.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only person to sign all four of the major founding documents: Continental Association, Declaration of Independence, Articles of Confederation, and U.S. Constitution.
Robert Morris (Pennsylvania)
Financier of the American Revolution. Helped fund the Continental Army.

These men were part of a group of 56 delegates who signed the Declaration in July and August 1776. Their signatures marked a historic break from British rule and laid the foundation for the United States.
