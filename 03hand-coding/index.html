The Gaming franchise From 1996 to 2026

Resident Evil is a Japanese horror media franchise created by Capcom in 1996. It began as a survival horror video game series and has since grown into a multimedia juggernaut, including movies, animated films, comics, novels, and merchandise. Its widely credited with popularizing and defining the survival horror genre.

Who are the Key members behind of the Resident evil game franchise

Shinji Mikami

Director and creator of the first Resident Evil game. Often called the "father of Resident Evil," Mikami defined the series' survival horror gameplay, fixed camera angles, and tense atmosphere. He later reinvented the series with Resident Evil 4's over-the-shoulder action style.

Tokuro Fujiwara

Producer of the original game. Fujiwara came up with the idea of creating a new horror game inspired by Capcomâ€™s earlier title Sweet Home, and he entrusted Shinji Mikami with bringing that vision to life.

Masami Ueda, Makoto Tomozawa, and others

The composers who created Resident Evil's haunting and atmospheric soundtrack, which helped define its distinctive horror feel and mood.

Isao Ohishi, Jun Takeuchi, and others

Key programmers, designers, and planners who built the game's complex puzzles, resource management systems, and iconic environments such as the Spencer Mansion.

Kenichi Iwao

Scenario writer responsible for developing much of the original game's lore, including the creation of the Umbrella Corporation, the T-virus, and the backstories of the main characters.

Hideki Kamiya

Director of Resident Evil 2, who expanded the series' story and introduced beloved characters like Leon S. Kennedy and Claire Redfield, further cementing Resident Evil's place in gaming history.

Hiroyuki Kobayashi

Programmer and later producer on several Resident Evil titles, contributing to the technical and creative direction of the series over the years.

Koshi Nakanishi

Director of Resident Evil 7: Biohazard, which successfully brought the series back to its survival horror roots with a modern first-person perspective.

Morimasa Sato

Director of Resident Evil Village, who helped blend gothic horror elements with the modern style introduced in Resident Evil 7.
