List of all Resident Evil Games released sinced 1996

All main games for you to know

Resident Evil (Biohazard) - 1996

Released for the PlayStation in March 1996 (later ported to other platforms), Resident Evil is a survival horror game created by Capcom, directed by Shinji Mikami. Its widely regarded as the game that defined the survival horror genre and made it mainstream.


In July 1998, strange murders occur on the outskirts of Raccoon City, near the Arklay Mountains. The citys elite police unit, S.T.A.R.S., sends the Bravo Team to investigate, but they lose contact. The Alpha Team (including Chris Redfield, Jill Valentine, Albert Wesker, and Barry Burton) is dispatched to find them. The team is attacked by monstrous dogs and takes refuge in a nearby mansion (the Spencer Mansion).

Resident Evil 2 - 1998

Released for the PlayStation in January 1998, Resident Evil 2 was directed by Hideki Kamiya and produced by Shinji Mikami. It expanded the scope of the original game and is often praised for its atmosphere, branching storylines, and unforgettable setting.


Set two months after the events of the first game, a viral outbreak has overrun Raccoon City, transforming most of its citizens into zombies. Two new protagonists arrive in the city on the same night

The palyer will choose to either play as Leon S Kennedy, a rookie cop on his first day at the Raccoon City Police Department or Claire Redfield a college student searching for her missing brother, Chris Redfield (one of the heroes from the first game).

Resident Evil 3: Nemesis - 1999

Released for the PlayStation in September 1999, Resident Evil 3: Nemesis was directed by Kazuhiro Aoyama and produced by Shinji Mikami. It takes place in the same city and timeframe as Resident Evil 2, expanding the story of the Raccoon City outbreak.


The game follows Jill Valentine, one of the protagonists from the original Resident Evil, as she attempts to escape Raccoon City, which has fallen into chaos after the T-virus outbreak.

Resident Evil – Code: Veronica - 2000

Released first on the Sega Dreamcast in February 2000, Code: Veronica was directed by Yoshiki Okamoto and produced by Shinji Mikami. It’s notable for being the first Resident Evil game rendered entirely in real-time 3D graphics rather than using pre-rendered backgrounds.


Set three months after Resident Evil 2, the story follows Claire Redfield, who is still searching for her brother Chris Redfield.

Resident Evil Code: Veronica X - 2001

Released in 2001 (for PlayStation 2 and later GameCube), Resident Evil Code: Veronica X is an enhanced version of Resident Evil Code: Veronica (2000). It’s largely the same game, but includes: New cutscenes that expand on the conflict between Albert Wesker and Chris Redfield. Small tweaks and visual improvements to the original Dreamcast version. The game remains part of the main Resident Evil storyline and is often seen as the true continuation of the saga after Resident Evil 2 and 3.

Resident Evil (Remake) – GameCube - 2002

Released exclusively for the Nintendo GameCube in March 2002, this remake of the original 1996 Resident Evil was directed by Shinji Mikami himself. It’s widely regarded as one of the best video game remakes ever made, thanks to its atmospheric graphics and expanded content.


The story is the same core premise as the 1996 original but reimagined with richer lore and new surprises: In July 1998, strange murders occur outside Raccoon City, in the Arklay Mountains. The special police unit S.T.A.R.S. sends two teams: Bravo Team, who go missing, and Alpha Team, who investigates. Chris Redfield, Jill Valentine, Albert Wesker, and Barry Burton from Alpha Team take refuge in the eerie Spencer Mansion after being attacked by monstrous dogs

Resident Evil Zero - 2002

Released for the Nintendo GameCube in November 2002, Resident Evil Zero is a prequel to the original Resident Evil. Directed by Koji Oda, it explores what happened to the S.T.A.R.S. Bravo Team just before the events at the Spencer Mansion.


Set in July 1998, Resident Evil Zero follows: Rebecca Chambers – the young and brilliant field medic of the S.T.A.R.S. Bravo Team. Billy Coen – an escaped convict and ex-Marine on death row for allegedly murdering 23 people. The story begins when Bravo Team’s helicopter crash-lands in the Arklay Mountains while investigating strange murders. Rebecca stumbles upon: The Ecliptic Express, a seemingly abandoned train that has been overrun by zombies and mutant leeches. Onboard, she encounters Billy Coen, and despite initial mistrust, they form an uneasy partnership to survive.

Resident Evil 4 - 2005

Released in January 2005 for the Nintendo GameCube (and later ported to PlayStation 2, PC, and many other platforms), Resident Evil 4 was directed by Shinji Mikami. It marked a dramatic reinvention of the series, blending survival horror with action gameplay and pioneering the iconic over-the-shoulder camera.


Set in 2004, six years after the events of Resident Evil 2, the game follows: Leon S. Kennedy – now a U.S. government agent. Leon’s mission: Travel to a remote rural region of Spain to rescue Ashley Graham, the kidnapped daughter of the U.S. President. Leon soon discovers: The local villagers, known as the Ganado, are controlled by a mind-controlling parasite called Las Plagas. The kidnapping was orchestrated by a cult called Los Illuminados, led by the mysterious Osmund Saddler, who plans to use Ashley to infect and control the U.S. government.

Resident Evil 5 - 2009

Released in March 2009 for PlayStation 3, Xbox 360, and PC, Resident Evil 5 was directed by Yasuhiro Anpo and Kenichi Ueda. It’s best known for expanding the series’ action-oriented gameplay and introducing full co-op mechanics.


Set in 2009, the story follows: Chris Redfield – now an agent for the BSAA (Bioterrorism Security Assessment Alliance). Sheva Alomar – his new partner, a BSAA agent from West Africa. Their mission takes them to the fictional Kijuju region in Africa, where they investigate reports of bio-organic weapons (BOWs) being sold on the black market.

Resident Evil 6 - 2012

Released in October 2012 for PlayStation 3, Xbox 360, and PC (later for PS4 and Xbox One), Resident Evil 6 was directed by Eiichiro Sasaki. It’s the most ambitious entry in the series, blending action and horror across four interwoven campaigns featuring seven playable characters.


Leon S. Kennedy & Helena Harper After the U.S. President is infected and mutates into a zombie, Leon and Secret Service agent Helena Harper uncover a conspiracy tied to Derek C. Simmons, a high-ranking government official. They fight to stop the C-virus outbreak in the U.S. and later in the fictional Chinese city of Lanshiang.


Chris Redfield & Piers Nivans Chris, traumatized after losing his team to a bioterror attack in Eastern Europe, returns to lead the BSAA. Together with Piers, they confront the J'avo (mutating C-virus enemies) and discover that Ada Wong (or someone impersonating her) is connected to the attack.


Jake Muller & Sherry Birkin Jake, a mercenary and the son of Albert Wesker, teams up with Sherry Birkin, now a government agent. Jake's blood holds the key to creating a vaccine against the C-virus. Their campaign combines escape and survival as they’re pursued by the unstoppable bio-weapon Ustanak.


Ada Wong (solo campaign, unlocked) Ada works to stop the plans of her clone — Carla Radames, a scientist who uses the C-virus to take revenge on the world. Ada’s story ties together the three other campaigns and reveals the true mastermind behind the outbreaks.

Resident Evil 7: Biohazard - 2017

Released in January 2017 for PlayStation 4, Xbox One, and PC (later for Switch and VR platforms), Resident Evil 7 was directed by Kōshi Nakanishi. It marked a dramatic return to survival horror roots, introducing a first-person perspective and a new storyline.


Set in 2017, the game follows: Ethan Winters, an ordinary man searching for his missing wife Mia, who disappeared three years earlier. Ethan receives a mysterious message from Mia, leading him to the derelict Baker family estate in Dulvey, Louisiana.

Resident Evil 2 (Remake)

Released in January 2019 for PlayStation 4, Xbox One, and PC (later for PS5 and Series X/S), this remake was directed by Kazunori Kadoi and Yasuhiro Anpo. It completely rebuilt the 1998 classic with a modern third-person, over-the-shoulder perspective while staying faithful to the spirit of the original.


Set in September 1998, during the height of the T-virus outbreak in Raccoon City, the game follows two protagonists whose stories run parallel: Leon S. Kennedy – a rookie police officer reporting for his first day at the Raccoon City Police Department (RPD). Claire Redfield – a college student searching for her missing brother, Chris Redfield. After a chaotic arrival, Leon and Claire are forced to split up but both make their way to the RPD, which is overrun by zombies.

Resident Evil 3 (Remake) - 2019

Released in April 2020 for PlayStation 4, Xbox One, and PC (later for PS5 and Xbox Series X/S), the Resident Evil 3 remake was directed by Kiyohiko Sakata and Yasuhiro Anpo. It reimagines the 1999 classic Resident Evil 3: Nemesis, built in the RE Engine like the Resident Evil 2 remake.


Set in September 1998, during the catastrophic T-virus outbreak in Raccoon City, the story follows: Jill Valentine, former S.T.A.R.S. officer, who is trying to escape the collapsing city. Carlos Oliveira, a mercenary in Umbrella’s U.B.C.S. (Umbrella Biohazard Countermeasure Service), sent to rescue survivors. The central threat is: The relentless Nemesis, a bio-weapon created by Umbrella to hunt and kill remaining S.T.A.R.S. members.

Resident Evil Village (Resident Evil 8)

Released in May 2021 for PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One, Xbox Series X/S, and PC, Resident Evil Village is the direct sequel to Resident Evil 7: Biohazard. Directed by Morimasa Sato, it continues Ethan Winters’ story in a gothic, folklore-inspired European setting.


Set three years after RE7, Ethan Winters is living a quiet life in Europe with his wife Mia and their newborn daughter Rose. Their peace is shattered when: Chris Redfield unexpectedly storms their home, appears to kill Mia, and kidnaps Ethan and Rose. Ethan awakens after the transport is attacked and finds himself in a mysterious, isolated Eastern European village, surrounded by monsters and ruled by four powerful lords under the enigmatic Mother Miranda.

Resident Evil 4 (Remake) - 2023

Released in March 2023 for PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X/S, and PC, the remake of Resident Evil 4 was directed by Yasuhiro Anpo and Kazunori Kadoi. It faithfully retells the story of the 2005 classic while updating gameplay, visuals, and narrative to modern standards.


Set in 2004, six years after the Raccoon City incident, the game follows: Leon S. Kennedy, now a U.S. government agent assigned to rescue Ashley Graham, the kidnapped daughter of the U.S. President. Leon travels to a remote rural region in Spain, where he discovers the local villagers are controlled by a parasite known as Las Plagas.
